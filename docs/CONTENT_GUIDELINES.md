## 📖 SCRIPTURE REQUIREMENTS (STRICT)

### MUST Use: Douay-Rheims Bible (Public Domain)

**Why Douay-Rheims?**
- Oldest Catholic English translation (1609/1633)
- Public Domain — free to quote
- True to Latin Vulgate
- Child-appropriate language
- No copyright restrictions

**Where to Find:**
- Douay-Rheims Full Text: www.catholic.com/daily-readings
- BibleGateway.com (select Douay-Rheims)
- Gutenberg Project (Project Gutenberg)
- Local library (printed Douay-Rheims Bible)

### Rules: Quote WORD-FOR-WORD

✅ **CORRECT:**
```
"Blessed are the poor in spirit: for theirs is the kingdom of heaven." — Matthew 5:3
```

❌ **INCORRECT:**
- Paraphrasing: "Jesus said those with humble hearts will go to heaven"
- Using other translation: "Blessed are the poor in spirit, for the kingdom of heaven is theirs" (Modern version)
- Changing punctuation: "Blessed are the poor in spirit. For theirs is the kingdom of heaven."

### Exact Format Required

**Book Chapter:Verse (NOT Chapter:Verse-Verse)**

✅ Correct:
- Matthew 5:3
- 1 Peter 3:3-4 (range is OK)
- Psalm 23:1

❌ Incorrect:
- Mt. 5:3 (abbreviation)
- Matthew 5, verse 3
- Matthew 5:3-4a

## ✝️ SAINT FACTS & STORIES

### Verified Sources ONLY

✅ **ACCEPTABLE:**
1. **Scripture** (Douay-Rheims)
   - Direct quote from Bible
   - Paraphrased with "[paraphrased]" note
   
2. **Public-Domain Catholic Works**
   - Lives of the Saints (Alban Butler, 1756)
   - The Life of Saint Francis (approved works)
   - Vatican documents
   - Saint canonization records (official)

3. **Original Writing**
   - Your own knowledge
   - Your own interpretation
   - Your own moral lesson

4. **Contemporary Verified Sources**
   - Catholic Encyclopedia (1913 — Public Domain)
   - SAINT.EWTN.COM (with permission/attribution)
   - Vatican.va official resources

❌ **DO NOT USE:**
- Modern Catholic books (copyright protected)
- Wikipedia articles (could be inaccurate)
- Random blog posts
- Second-hand retellings without source
- Paraphrased facts without original source
- Modern movies/dramatizations as fact
- Guesses or assumptions

### Fact-Checking Process

**Before writing each saint entry:**

1. **Confirm the saint exists**
   - Is this an actual saint or blessed?
   - What feast day? (January 2, December 25, etc.)

2. **Find primary sources**
   - Scripture reference
   - Official canonization record
   - Saint biography

3. **Verify the story**
   - Check multiple sources
   - Confirm dates and names
   - Note any conflicting accounts

4. **Write original version**
   - Use your own words (not copied)
   - Highlight ONE virtue only
   - Make it child-appropriate & engaging

5. **Double-check Scripture**
   - Quote verse EXACTLY from Douay-Rheims
   - Verify book/chapter/verse reference
   - Test that verse matches the saint's story

## 👶 CHILD-APPROPRIATE CONTENT

### Age Range: 6-12 Years Old

**Language:**
- Simple, clear sentences
- Avoid complex theology
- Use concrete examples children understand
- Explain virtues in relatable ways

**Tone:**
- Warm and encouraging
- Not scary or threatening
- Hopeful and positive
- Emphasize God's love

**Content Restrictions:**

❌ **DO NOT INCLUDE:**
- Graphic violence (even historical)
- Sexual content (any kind)
- Scary/nightmares imagery
- Detailed suffering descriptions
- Modern slang/inappropriate language
- Theology too advanced for children
- Divisive political views
- Doubt-inducing questions about faith

✅ **OK TO INCLUDE:**
- Courage in face of difficulty
- Sacrifice/giving up things for God
- Miracles and answered prayers
- Saints who lived simply
- Historical details (age-appropriate)
- Questions about faith that build understanding
- Acts of kindness & service
- Ancient customs (explained simply)

### Examples

**❌ Bad (Too Graphic):**
"Saint Sebastian was shot with arrows by Roman soldiers and died bleeding on the ground."

**✅ Good (Age-Appropriate):**
"Saint Sebastian was brave even when bad things happened. He knew Jesus loved him, and that gave him courage."

## 📚 SOURCING SAINT STORIES

### Example: Saint George (April 23)

**1. Confirm he's real:**
- Yes, Saint George is a real saint
- Feast day: April 23

**2. Find primary sources:**
- Catholic Encyclopedia (1913, Public Domain)
- Vatican hagiography
- Church tradition

**3. Verify the dragon story:**
- Legend (not historically verified fact)
- BUT: Symbolizes overcoming evil (virtue teaching)
- OK to use with understanding it's a legend

**4. Select a virtue:**
- Courage
- Faithfulness
- Perseverance

**5. Write original version:**
"Long ago in a land far away, there was a soldier named George who loved Jesus very much. The people in his town were very scared because they believed a fierce dragon was threatening them. But George was not afraid, because he knew Jesus was stronger than any dragon. He prayed and asked Jesus for courage..."

**6. Link to Scripture:**
Selected: 2 Timothy 1:7 (Douay-Rheims)
"For God hath not given us the spirit of fear; but of power, and of love, and of a sound mind."

- Popular Catholic books

- Use your own words

## 🚫 COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### 1. Wrong Bible Translation

❌ **WRONG:**
"Blessed are those who are down on their luck, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven" (modern paraphrase)

✅ **RIGHT:**
"Blessed are the poor in spirit: for theirs is the kingdom of heaven." (Douay-Rheims, Matthew 5:3)

### 2. Copied Stories

❌ **WRONG:**
Copy paragraph directly from "Lives of Saints" book

✅ **RIGHT:**
Read the source, then write in your own words

### 3. Unverified Facts

❌ **WRONG:**
"Saint X invented [thing]" (without confirmation)

✅ **RIGHT:**
"(Content needed — add verified facts here)" — then research

### 4. Age-Inappropriate Content

❌ **WRONG:**
"Saint X was tortured to death in horrible ways..."

✅ **RIGHT:**
"Saint X suffered bravely because he loved Jesus..."

### 5. Wrong Scripture Reference

❌ **WRONG:**
"Matthew 3:22" (doesn't exist)

✅ **RIGHT:**
Verify verse exists: Matthew 3:17

### 6. Overly Complex Language

❌ **WRONG:**
"The theological implications of sanctification manifest through ecclesiastical virtue..."

✅ **RIGHT:**
"Being holy means being close to God and doing what's right."
